The Rehearsed Lie

February 21, 2026 • 3 min read

We stood by the edge of the water, where the city lights bled into the dark river like spilled ink. You asked me if I was happy, and I paused—not because I didn't know the answer, but because I was searching for the version of it that wouldn't hurt you.

It is a strange thing, the rehearsed lie. It sits on the tongue like a smooth stone, polished by anxiety and the need to please. I told you "yes," and the word floated between us, weightless and hollow.

The truth is often too sharp to hold. We wrap it in soft words, in smiles that don't quite reach our eyes, until it feels like mercy. But mercy for whom? For the one hearing the lie, or the one too coward to speak the truth?

I watched the ripples distort the reflection of the moon. In that moment, I realized that we were both just reflections—distorted versions of ourselves, shimmering on the surface, terrified of the depth beneath.
